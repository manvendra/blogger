History

World without time zones

By 300 BCE, western world agreed that the earth is round. Each developed civilization devised its unique distinct system to measure distances, times & absolute locations, but relative to prime locations within their civilizations.

It all worked in ancient times because, long-distance travel was not prevalent among common people. Only merchants or armies frequently traveled long distances. And they already developed some system that worked on their predetermined routes, irrespective of the time differences between locations.

Colonialism Needs & Funding

Little past medieval ages, Imperial powers looked towards maritime for empire expansions. They started venturing into unknown routes to find better trade routes & new conquerable territories.

As the insatiable western greed increased, so did the requirement for a better navigation system. Kings, rich merchants, and wealthy investors started showing interest in improving navigation methods, which could increase the scope for expansion and reliability of profits.

This western imperialism and commercial expansion led to the foundation of observatories, committees, and science schools, who among many other goal also had the goal to successfully identify longitudes.

Prominent Early Institutions

After Columbus discovered the new world, the Spanish empire set up Casa de la ContractaciÃ³n at Seville in 1503. It was the very first databank of all information collected from earlier navigators/travelers. This information was used to develop charts as well as navigation instruments.

Century later, the other two colonial superpowers France & England set up observatories in Paris in 1667 and Greenwich in 1675 respectively.

All of these agencies marked the earth for navigation. Principles for markings were the same but the end products were not.

International Meridian Conference (IMC)

By the late 1800s, another colonial superpower was rising in the far west. The 13 colonies on the east coast of this newly discovered continent, which started as a free country called America, now owned a vast land, stretching from the Atlantic to the Pacific and far beyond.

This new country was so vast that the sunrise in one corner is the deep dark on the other side. With the invention of the Railways, the problems created by time differences were compounded.

In 1884 America invited all great minds from the world to establish a universal prime meridian & uniform standard times, These bunch of old white men in the below picture, formulated the rules that govern how the whole world sees the time to this day.

The Science (sort of)

These wise men passed a few resolutions in International Meridian Conference which directly or indirectly decide how we observe the time.

A simple interpretation of IMC resolutions

  • A universal solar day is beginning at midnight in Greenwich and is counted on a 24-hour clock
  • All nautical and astronomical days everywhere begin at 12:00 a.m.
  • Meridians are imaginary circles passing through the geographic poles. The Meridian passing through Greenwich Observatory England is considered the prime meridian.
  • Longitudes are the angular distance measured west or east of the prime meridian.
  • The timezone at the prime meridian is called Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). Time here is known as Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).

Conclusion

Before I conclude this topic, to leave your brain ringing, one last location I want to talk about is North-South Poles. 
As we saw previously, all longitudes converge at the north and the South poles. So in theory, all timezones are valid on the poles. In other words, poles lie in all time zones. :-)
